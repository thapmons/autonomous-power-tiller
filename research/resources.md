# 📚 Research & Resources

This document tracks the foundational research, projects, and media that inform the development of this autonomous power tiller framework.

## 📄 Academic Research
* **"Mechanisation of small-scale farms in South Asia: Empirical evidence"** *Authors: Jeetendra Aryal, Dil Rahut, et al.* [Read on ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/350947921_Mechanisation_of_small-scale_farms_in_South_Asia_Empirical_evidence_derived_from_farm_households_survey)  
  *Key Insight:* Highlighting the massive shift from animal power to 2-wheel tractors (power tillers) in Bangladesh and India.

* **"Modeling and simulation of power tiller for autonomous operation"** *Authors: Lasitha Piyathilaka, et al.* [Read on IEEE Xplore/ResearchGate](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/224132598_Modeling_and_simulation_of_power_tiller_for_autonomous_operation_in_agricultural_fields)  
  *Key Insight:* Detailed kinematics and dynamics for creating a "Digital Twin" of a power tiller in a simulator.

## 🛠️ Open Source Projects
* **Open Source Ecology: MicroTrac** [Official Project Link](https://www.opensourceecology.org/microtrac-challenge/)  
  *Description:* A collaborative challenge to build a small-scale open-source tractor. Their "modular" approach to hardware is a major inspiration for this project's retrofit kit.

* **ROS2 (Robot Operating System)** [Documentation](https://docs.ros.org/en/humble/index.html)  
  *Description:* The industry-standard middleware for building robot brains.

## 📺 Media & Visual References
* **Iron Buffalo: The 2-Wheel Tractor in Action** [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Gg84MAwKlCU)  
  *Why it matters:* This video provides a mechanical breakdown of the "Thai-style" tractors that dominate the region—essential for understanding the steering physics.

* **Extreme Puddling: Power Tillers in Deep Mud** [Watch on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=J_gJPBZXalg)  
  *Why it matters:* Visualizing the "worst-case scenario" for computer vision sensors (mud splatter, vibration, and low-traction environments).
